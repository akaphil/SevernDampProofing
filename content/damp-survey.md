## Damp Surveys

A good survey is the most important part of any damp job. Get the diagnosis right and the fix is usually straightforward; get it wrong and you pay for treatments that don't work. We take the survey seriously.

### What a Proper Survey Involves

<div class="feature-grid">
<div class="feature-box">
<h4>Thorough Inspection</h4>
<p>We examine the affected areas inside and out — pointing, render, gutters, downpipes, ground levels, ventilation and the damp-proof course.</p>
</div>
<div class="feature-box feature-box--gold">
<h4>Readings & Analysis</h4>
<p>Moisture readings and, where relevant, salt analysis to distinguish genuine rising damp from the far more common penetrating damp and condensation.</p>
</div>
<div class="feature-box feature-box--slate">
<h4>Clear Written Report</h4>
<p>A plain-English report explaining what's causing the damp, what we recommend, and what it will cost — no jargon, no pressure.</p>
</div>
</div>

### Why It Matters

The damp-proofing trade has a reputation for reaching for the most expensive "solution" — usually a chemical damp-proof course — regardless of the real cause. We don't work that way. Often the honest answer is a repair to pointing, a cleared gutter or better ventilation, not a costly treatment. If that's what you need, that's what we'll tell you.
