## Basement & Cellar Tanking

Below-ground rooms are in constant contact with damp ground, so they need a deliberate waterproofing strategy. Done well, a damp cellar becomes a dry, usable space — storage, a utility room, a workshop or extra living space.

### Two Proven Approaches

<div class="feature-grid">
<div class="feature-box feature-box--gold">
<h4>Cementitious Tanking</h4>
<p>A multi-coat waterproof render/slurry bonded directly to the walls and floor, sealing the structure against water ingress. Ideal where a solid, plasterable finish is wanted.</p>
</div>
<div class="feature-box feature-box--slate">
<h4>Cavity-Drain Membrane</h4>
<p>A studded membrane lines the walls and floor, channelling any water to a drainage point or sump and pump. A reliable, maintainable system, especially for conversions.</p>
</div>
</div>

We recommend the right system — or combination — for your structure, water conditions and how you intend to use the room.

### What's Involved

1. **Survey & assessment** — we establish the water conditions, the structure, and how the space will be used.
2. **Preparation** — removing old finishes and failed tanking, preparing sound surfaces.
3. **Waterproofing** — installing the tanking or membrane system to specification.
4. **Finishing** — battening, boarding or plastering ready for your intended use.
